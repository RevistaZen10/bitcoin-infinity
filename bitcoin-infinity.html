01 ‚Äî The Problem

Bitcoin's
Great Filter.

The greatest threat to Bitcoin's future is not regulation, competition, or quantum computing.
It is a simple, unavoidable biological fact: people die.

The original Bitcoin operates on the dangerous assumption that the transfer of private keys from one generation to the next will be a flawless process. A ~132-year cycle spans practically two full human generations ‚Äî imposing a near-impossible condition: for an asset to remain in circulation, its private key must be successfully inherited not once, but twice.
